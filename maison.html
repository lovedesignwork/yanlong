A kitchen of Chengdu,

食 之 有 道

"We are a very small house
with a very long memory."

When Chef Wei Jianhong opened Yan Long in 2019, he was fifty-four years old and had already spent thirty years in kitchens he did not own. He brought with him one recipe book, handwritten, and a carbon steel wok seasoned since 1998.

The house he founded — on the first floor of the Royal Phuket City Hotel — does not seat more than ninety. It does not serve fusion. It does not use a gas-pressure fryer. The techniques are the ones his mother taught him, at the temperatures his grandmother taught her.

L'Atelier · The House

Three quiet
inheritances.

Every house keeps a handful of inherited gestures. Ours are three. The first is a breath — wok hei — the split-second alchemy of fire and oil. The second is a hand, folding. The third is patience: a duck, brined for two days before it is ever touched.
